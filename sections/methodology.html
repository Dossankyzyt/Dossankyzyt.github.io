How We Identified the Gaps

Our project began by recognizing that structured data platforms like Wikidata often contain incomplete or inconsistent representations of real-world entities. Specifically, we focused on the city of Tver, where key geographic and political details (like rivers and mayors) were missing or incomplete.

General Methodology

We applied a systematic approach to detect and enrich data gaps in Wikidata:

  • 🔍 Used SPARQL queries to check which entities were linked to Tver.
  • 💬 Prompted LLMs (ChatGPT and Gemini) to verify missing rivers and mayors.
  • 🔗 Located the correct QIDs of rivers (e.g., Tvertsa: Q671890, Tmaka: Q4467262).
  • 📊 Generated RDF triples to propose new structured entries.

Tools Used

  • 🗺️ Wikidata: Structured knowledge graph where data was queried and enriched.
  • 🔎 SPARQL: For retrieving and validating links between entities.
  • 🤖 Large Language Models (LLMs): ChatGPT and Gemini to identify omissions and generate data suggestions.
  • 🧠 Zero-shot prompting: Used for generating RDF triples and identifying missing civic/geographic data.
